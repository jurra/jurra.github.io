My Design Approach

Be agile but systematic, be concurrent but structured, don't be afraid to experiment and innovate, but confront your assumptions and brightest ideas with reality checks

Within the classical spheres of technology, human factors and economy I combine in a concurrent manner the following design activities.

domain driven design

I analyze the domain and context in which I design, using the activity modeling framework. Activity Modeling is a tool to model the context and human activity beyond individual users. It also has notation that allows to specify with minimum ambiguities requirements and implications for different actors. It is a very powerful tool to get the most out of interviews and workshops with users, but also to frame user stories in a consistent manner and conceptulize customer journeys, among other things.

value proposition design

I use Value Proposition Design to match problems and opportunities with regard to a new experience and improved workflows. It is more of a strategy framework to align activity centered analysis and design, with the business and organizational context. I use it contextualize design improvements and minimum viable products, that are not only desirable but also feasible and viable.

design for interaction and implementation

This is the most "techy" work I do, making sure that there is a choherent implementation of desired user experiences and value propositions. From this area or design domaing, I try to understand and put into context technological and architectural opportunities, pearing up with architects and engineers. UI is about materializing these goals and requirements unfolded from Activity Centered Design, value proposition design and putting technology into the context of the particular project. Here is where sketching, prototyping and testing come into play. This is also where most of the specfications are clear enough to be transfered to front-end development and get user centered design in the pipeline of scrum sprints.
