Work

Every design project has a bit of strategy, good taste, technology and even religion, I run the risk of simplifying my work with these bold categories to gain in clearness

Strategic Product Design coming soon


Most common deliverables I end up with in these projects are opportunities and vision documentation using roadmaps, value propositions, and business model canvases within the framework of lean startup. These results are always grounded on in depth research related to the domain of the project.

UX for Digital Products see the projects


When designing for digital experiences I first map and model the most relevant interactions and workflows, ending up with critical user stories. I also profile customers/users segments using personas. The most materialized deliverables in this context are visually documented user stories, backed up by tests and evaluations, ready to enter the development pipeline.

Industrial Design see the projects


I think of Industrial Design as UX for physical products.Human centeredness is the dominant element also for this projects grounded by feasibility. Although deliverables change(CAD models, prototypes, simulations, calculations, usability tests), the approach in my experience remains very similar as when designing for digital contexts.

Visual Design see the projects


This is the most “artistic” work I do, including graphic design, illustration and also 3D modeling. The major focus of these projects has been on making products that look and feel good, but also engage in delivering emotional impact. Every project has a little bit of this, but these collection is all about it.
